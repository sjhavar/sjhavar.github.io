MY JOURNEY

To me fitness is much more than weight lifting, calisthenics, and cardio. With gains that surpass being pumped, ripped, and sweaty. Fitness became my light, my rock, and salvation at a time when life was dark, unstable, and chaotic. I grew up in a single parent home of seven, in a crime stricken town in Boston where options were limited and positive role models scarce. Scare became extinct once the single parent home I grew up in became a zero parent home. I lost my mother at the age of fifteen and with her went my desire to live. In reverie I lost my mother way before she passed away on the operating table. She was a type one diabetic with high blood pressure and high cholesterol and the daily medication she was prescribed slowly ate the life of her. It took a while for me to come to terms with her death but once accepted I refused to succumb to the same tragedy. That’s when fitness became my therapist and the gym became that long comfortable sofa where I let it all out. Everyone struggles and faces adversity at some point in life and holding it in does much more harm than good. Fitness was how I released that stress and I’m here to help others experience the beauty I found through fitness.
